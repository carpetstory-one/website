# Why Eight Months

We often get asked why a single rug takes the better part of a year to weave. In an era of next-day delivery and instant gratification, telling a client that their piece will be ready in late autumn can feel jarring. 

But true luxury is inextricably linked to time.

<Aside>
At 100 knots per square inch, an 8x10 rug contains roughly 1.15 million individual knots.
</Aside>

## The Mathematics of Patience

Consider an 8x10 foot rug woven at our standard density of 100 knots per square inch (KPSI). This translates to 14,400 knots per square foot, or approximately 1,152,000 knots for the entire piece. 

A master weaver, working rhythmically and without interruption, can tie about 5,000 to 6,000 knots in a single day. When multiple weavers sit at the loom—typically three for an 8-foot width—they progress at roughly one to two inches vertically per day. 

> "We do not measure progress in days, but in motifs completed."

## Beyond the Loom

The weaving itself accounts for only five or six months. Before a single knot is tied, the wool must be carded, spun by hand, and dyed in small batches over open fires to achieve the perfect abrash (the natural variation in color).

Once the rug comes off the loom, it undergoes a rigorous finishing process:
- **Washing:** Repeated washing with wooden paddles to bring out the luster of the wool.
- **Stretching:** Tensioning the wet rug on a frame for days to ensure it dries perfectly square.
- **Shearing:** Hand-shearing the pile to an even height, revealing the crispness of the design.

There is no shortcut to this process. Any attempt to accelerate it compromises the integrity, durability, and soul of the carpet. Eight months is not a delay; it is the prerequisite for a masterpiece.
